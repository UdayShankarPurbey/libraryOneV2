Code of Conduct

The Gandhi Institute for Education and Technology (GIET) Library is committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and respectful environment for all members of our community. This Code of Conduct outlines the standards of behavior expected from all users of the library.

1. Respectful Behavior

a. Treat all library users and staff with respect and courtesy.
b. Maintain a quiet environment conducive to study and research.
c. Refrain from disruptive behavior, including loud conversations, use of mobile phones, and playing music without headphones.

2. Use of Library Resources

a. Handle all library materials with care and return them on time.
b. Do not mutilate, deface, or damage any library materials or property.
c. Use library computers and electronic resources responsibly and in accordance with the library's policies.

3. Personal Belongings

a. Keep personal belongings with you at all times. The library is not responsible for lost or stolen items.
b. Do not leave personal belongings unattended to reserve a study space.

4. Food and Drink

a. Only bottled water is allowed in the library. No other food or drink is permitted.
b. Dispose of all waste in the designated bins.

5. Library Membership

a. Library membership is required to borrow materials. Ensure your membership is current and up-to-date.
b. Report any loss of library cards immediately to avoid misuse.

6. Internet and Computer Use

a. Use of the internet and computers must comply with the library's Acceptable Use Policy.
b. Accessing inappropriate or offensive content is strictly prohibited.

7. Consequences of Violations

a. Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in suspension or revocation of library privileges.
b. Serious offenses may be reported to the appropriate authorities.

By using the GIET Library, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct. We thank you for your cooperation in maintaining a positive and respectful environment for all.
